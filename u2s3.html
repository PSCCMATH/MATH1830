Derivatives of Products and Quotients

Rewriting a Function as a Product or Quotient

  1. Rewrite as a product: $f\left( x \right) = 5{e^x}\; + \;\;10{x^2}\;{e^x}\; + \;\;25{x^4}\;{e^x}$

    $f(x) = 5{e^x}(1 + 2{x^2} + 5{x^4})$

  2. Rewrite as one quotient: $f\left( x \right) = 3{x^{ - 4}}\ln x$

    $f(x) = \frac{{3\ln x}}{{{x^4}}}$

    The Product Rule

    If $y=f(x)\cdot g(x)$,

    then $y' = f'(x)\cdot g(x)\; + \;\;f(x)\cdot g'(x)$

    The Quotient Rule

    If $y = \;\frac{f(x)}{g(x)},$

    then $y' = \;\frac{{f'(x)\;g(x)\; - \;f(x)\;g'(x)\;}}{{[{g(x)}}]^2}$

    Two Methods for Finding the Derivative:

    Find the derivative two different ways.

    1. Simplify first and use the power rule.
    2. Use the product or quotient rule.
